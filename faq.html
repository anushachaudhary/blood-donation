Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

Blood donation is the process of voluntarily giving blood to help those in need, typically during surgeries, accidents, or medical treatments. Donated blood is used to save lives.

Individuals who are healthy, between the ages of 18-65, weigh at least 50kg (110 lbs), and have no history of blood-borne diseases are eligible to donate blood. However, eligibility may vary based on local regulations.

Yes, donating blood is a safe procedure. It is conducted under sterile conditions, and only one pint of blood is collected, which the body quickly replenishes.

You can donate whole blood every 56 days (8 weeks). For platelet donations, it’s typically every 7 days, but you must wait at least 14 days between each donation.

After donating blood, it’s important to rest for a few minutes, drink plenty of fluids, and have a healthy snack. Avoid strenuous activities for the rest of the day.
